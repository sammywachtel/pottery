Variation 3: Horizontal Scroll Cards

Layout Strategy

Single-column, full-width cards: Each card spans the full width with a horizontally scrollable photo container. Portrait and square photos center in the viewport (no scrolling needed), while landscape photos extend beyond the viewport and can be swiped left/right to see the full image. All photos maintain their natural aspect ratio.

How it handles aspect ratios:

Fixed 240px height for all photo containers. Width adapts to aspect ratio: portrait (180px), square (240px), landscape (extends beyond viewport). Uses scroll-snap for smooth photo viewing. Landscape photos show a subtle "swipe" indicator on the right edge.

✓ Pros

  • Zero cropping - all photos visible in full
  • Landscape photos get full width when scrolled
  • Natural mobile interaction (horizontal swipe)
  • Single-column layout = consistent vertical rhythm
  • Each item gets equal prominence
  • Large photo area for better viewing

✗ Cons

  • Shows fewer items per screen (vertical space)
  • Requires horizontal scroll gesture for landscapes
  • Two scroll directions can be confusing
  • Less efficient use of screen real estate
  • May slow down browsing (more scrolling)
  • Not a traditional grid layout
